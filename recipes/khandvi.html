Khandvi

Description

Khandvi is a popular Gujarati snack made from gram flour (besan) and yogurt, known for its delicate, rolled-up appearance and savory flavor. The batter is cooked until it reaches a smooth, spreadable consistency, then spread thinly on a surface to cool. Once set, it is cut into strips and rolled up tightly. Khandvi is typically seasoned with mustard seeds, sesame seeds, and garnished with fresh coriander. It’s a delightful snack that’s both light and flavorful, often enjoyed with green chutney.

Ingredients

For the batter

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 cups water
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-green chili paste
  • Salt to taste

For the tempering

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, yogurt, water, turmeric powder, ginger-green chili paste, and salt. Whisk until smooth and lump-free.
  2. Pour the mixture into a non-stick pan and cook on low heat, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Cook until the batter thickens and starts to leave the sides of the pan.
  3. Spread the cooked batter evenly on a greased surface or parchment paper. Let it cool for a few minutes until set.
  4. Once set, cut the batter into strips (about 1 inch wide) and roll each strip tightly from one end to the other.
  5. For tempering, heat oil in a small pan, add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add sesame seeds and asafoetida, then remove from heat.
  6. Drizzle the tempering over the rolled khandvi and garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  7. Serve immediately with green chutney or as a snack.
