Samosa

Description

Samosa is a popular Indian snack consisting of a crispy, golden-brown pastry filled with a spiced mixture of mashed potatoes, peas, and various herbs. The outer layer is made from all-purpose flour, rolled thin, and shaped into a triangular pocket before being deep-fried until perfectly crisp. Samosas are often served with tangy tamarind chutney or mint chutney, making them a delightful appetizer or tea-time snack that’s loved by many.

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 4 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • ½ teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed to knead the dough)

For the filling

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • ½ cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1–2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional for heat)
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, carom seeds, salt, and oil/ghee. Mix well until crumbly.
  2. Add water gradually and knead into a smooth, firm dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. For the filling, heat a little oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  4. Add the boiled potatoes, green peas, garam masala, coriander powder, green chilies, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  5. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval shape.
  6. Cut the oval in half to form two semi-circles. Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edge with water.
  7. Fill the cone with the potato-pea mixture and seal the open edge by pressing it firmly.
  8. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the samosas until golden brown and crisp on all sides.
  9. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with chutney or sauce of your choice.
